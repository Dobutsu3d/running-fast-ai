## VISUAL ARCHETYPES — 6 CATEGORIES

### A — DARK STUDIO PRODUCT
*The purest Nike format. Product isolated. No distractions.*

| File | Product | Key Technique |
|---|---|---|
| imgi_194 | Nike Alphafly (white/pink, pair) | Pure black bg. Two shoes, front + side angle. Product floating/levitating. No floor contact. No FX. Clean, maximum material read. |
| imgi_128 | Nike Air Zoom (white CGI) | Dark charcoal gradient bg. Single shoe lateral profile. Orange lightning/energy FX emanating from midsole. Product hovers above reflective floor plane. CGI render, not photography. |

**Standard for this category:**
- Background: `#111111` to `#000000` — pure black or near-black
- Product: floating, no floor shadow or shadow confined to ground contact only
- Lighting: rim light separating product from bg, accent on midsole geometry
- Format: 4:5 portrait or 9:16 vertical
- Text: product name bottom-left in small white sans-serif; Swoosh top-right if used

- Shoe occupies minimum 40% of frame and is always in focus (deep or sharp)

- Background: white cyclorama OR outdoor environment OR soft neutral gradient
- Model styling: athletic, clean, minimal — clothing serves the shoe, not the other way around

## MASTER VISUAL STANDARD — WHAT THIS MEANS FOR GENERATION

### The 4 non-negotiables across all categories:
1. **Product is always in focus and perfectly readable** — regardless of how abstract the treatment
2. **Portrait format is the default** — 4:5 or 9:16 (vertical). Landscape only for specific campaign banners.
3. **Product floats or is shot from below** — never placed sitting flat with eye-level camera
4. **One dominant visual statement per frame** — one energy FX, one color pop, one background treatment

### Background frequency from refs:
- Pure black / dark studio: **35%** of refs → primary format
- Gradient (dark-to-color): **15%** → campaign/highlight format
- White cyclorama: **15%** → athlete editorial and heritage
- Outdoor/environment: **20%** → action and atmospheric
- Abstract/painterly: **15%** → brand campaign

### Camera height from refs:
- Ground level (0–20cm): **60%** of refs
- Standard low (20–60cm): **30%**
- Eye level or above: **10%** — almost never used

### The consistent Nike camera angle rule:
> Nike shows shoes from below and in front — the athlete's perspective looking down at their own foot, or the ground looking up. Never a bystander's eye-level view.
